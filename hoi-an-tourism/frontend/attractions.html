Discover the magic and history of ancient Vietnam

🌉 Japanese Covered Bridge (Chùa Cầu)

Japanese Covered Bridge (Chùa Cầu)

📍 Nguyen Thi Minh Khai Street ⭐ 4.7/5 (2,340 reviews)

The iconic Japanese Covered Bridge, built in the 16th century, is Hoi An's most famous landmark. This beautiful wooden bridge connects the Japanese and Chinese quarters of the ancient town. Legend says it was built to kill a monster whose head, heart, and tail lay across Vietnam, India, and Japan respectively.

Highlights:

  • 🏛️ 400-year-old architectural masterpiece
  • 🎨 Beautiful carved decorations and statues
  • 📸 Perfect spot for photography
  • 🌙 Magical atmosphere during full moon festivals

Opening Hours: 24/7 (Free to cross)

Best Time to Visit: Early morning or evening for fewer crowds

Entry Fee: Free (included in Old Town ticket)

🏮 Hoi An Ancient Town

Hoi An Ancient Town

📍 Central Hoi An ⭐ 4.8/5 (5,678 reviews)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Hoi An Ancient Town is a remarkably well-preserved example of a Southeast Asian trading port from the 15th to 19th centuries. Its buildings and street plan reflect the influences of indigenous and foreign cultures over time.

Highlights:

  • 🏛️ 844 officially recognized historic buildings
  • 🏮 Colorful lanterns lining the streets
  • 🎭 Traditional craft workshops
  • 🍜 Authentic local restaurants and cafes

Opening Hours: 24/7

Ticket: Old Town Ticket required (120,000 VND)

Includes: Entry to 5 heritage sites of your choice

🛶 Thu Bon River

Thu Bon River

📍 Flows through Hoi An ⭐ 4.6/5 (1,892 reviews)

The Thu Bon River has been the lifeblood of Hoi An for centuries. Take a peaceful boat ride to experience the town from a different perspective, especially beautiful during sunset when the ancient buildings reflect in the calm waters.

Activities:

  • 🛶 Traditional basket boat tours
  • 🌅 Sunset river cruises
  • 🏮 Evening lantern boat rides
  • 🎣 Fishing experiences with locals

Best Time: Late afternoon for sunset views

Boat Tours: 150,000 - 300,000 VND per person

Duration: 1-2 hours depending on tour

🏖️ An Bang Beach

An Bang Beach

📍 4km from Hoi An center ⭐ 4.5/5 (967 reviews)

Just a short bike ride from Hoi An's ancient town, An Bang Beach offers pristine white sand and clear blue waters. This peaceful beach is perfect for relaxing after exploring the cultural sites, with fewer crowds than other Vietnamese beaches.

Features:

  • 🏖️ Clean, white sandy beach
  • 🏄‍♂️ Surfing and water sports
  • 🍹 Beachside bars and restaurants
  • 🚲 Easy bike ride from town

Distance: 4km from Ancient Town

Transportation: Bicycle (20 mins) or motorbike (10 mins)

Best Time: Morning or late afternoon

🏛️ My Son Sanctuary

My Son Sanctuary

📍 60km from Hoi An ⭐ 4.4/5 (1,234 reviews)

My Son Sanctuary is a cluster of abandoned and partially ruined Hindu temples constructed between the 4th and 14th centuries by the Champa civilization. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers insight into the ancient Cham culture and their impressive architectural skills.

Highlights:

  • 🏛️ Ancient Cham temple ruins (4th-14th century)
  • 🎭 Traditional Cham cultural performances
  • 🌿 Beautiful mountain and forest setting
  • 📚 Museum with Cham artifacts

Distance: 60km (1.5-hour drive) from Hoi An

Entry Fee: 150,000 VND

Best Time: Early morning (cooler weather)

🥬 Tra Que Village

Tra Que Vegetable Village

📍 3km from Hoi An center ⭐ 4.3/5 (578 reviews)

Experience authentic Vietnamese rural life at Tra Que Village, famous for its organic vegetables and herbs. Join a cooking class, work alongside local farmers, or simply bike through the peaceful countryside to see traditional farming methods.

Activities:

  • 🚲 Scenic bicycle tours through farms
  • 👨‍🌾 Hands-on farming experience
  • 🍳 Cooking classes with fresh ingredients
  • 🥗 Farm-to-table dining experiences

Distance: 3km (15-minute bike ride)

Tours: Half-day or full-day options available

Price: 200,000 - 400,000 VND per person
